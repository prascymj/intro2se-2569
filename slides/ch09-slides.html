Concept 1 / 5

Planning & estimation

Break work into small tasks and roughly estimate each. Small tasks are easier to estimate, assign, and finish — and progress becomes visible.

Example
“Build reminders” (too big) → “add Task model”, “write scheduler”, “write 2 tests” (each ~a few hours).
Intro2SE · Ch9
Concept 2 / 5

Sprints & boards

Work in short cycles (sprints) and track tasks on a board with columns To-do / Doing / Done. Limit how many things are “Doing” at once.

Example
StudyMate sprint 1 board: cards move left→right; everyone sees status without a meeting.
Intro2SE · Ch9
Concept 3 / 5

Risk awareness (light)

A risk is something that might go wrong. Name the top one or two early and plan a response — surprises hurt small teams most.

Example
Risk: “only one teammate knows Git.” Response: pair-program a Git session in week 6.
Intro2SE · Ch9
Concept 4 / 5

Roles & responsibility

Assign clear roles (and rotate them) so everyone contributes and no one is overloaded. Responsibility is individual, even in a team.

Example
Rotate who is “scribe/lead” each sprint; the contribution table records who did what.
Intro2SE · Ch9
Concept 5 / 5

The retrospective

At the end of a sprint, the team briefly asks: what went well, what didn't, what will we change? This is how teams improve.

Example
“Tests slowed us down” → next sprint, write the test right after the function, not at the end.
