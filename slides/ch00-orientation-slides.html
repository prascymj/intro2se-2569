The big question

If AI can write the code,
what is the engineer for?

This is the question the whole course answers. AI can generate code, tests, even documents — but it cannot decide what is worth building, judge trade-offs, or take responsibility.

Your job this term: frame the problem · decide · verify · be accountable · work as a team. AI is a fast assistant that you direct and check.
Intro2SE · Day 1 Orientation
What you'll do

Build one real project, as a team, all term

You'll go through the complete software process once: requirements → design → build → test → secure → present. Teams of about 5.

The project
Shared problem: helping a student organize their study life — schedules, deadlines, group work, notes, activities.
Everyone works in the same domain, but each team builds different features.

AI policy

You MAY use AI — with three rules

  • Log it — what you asked, what it gave you
  • Verify it — check and fix what it produced
  • Explain it — out loud, in the oral exam
The rule
Work you cannot explain earns no credit. “AI wrote it and I submitted it” will not pass.
Intro2SE · Day 1 Orientation
AI policy

Concretely

✓ OK
• ask AI to explain a concept
• let AI draft code, then read/test/fix it
• ask AI for test cases, then check coverage
• log all of it honestly
✗ Not OK
• submit AI output you don't understand
• pass off a teammate's work as yours
• hide or deny AI use
• let one person “AI” the whole project

Individual accountability

Two individual checks — why?

① The assignment (Wk 8). Summarise a tech article: one page + a ≤3-minute video of you presenting it. You must say it in your own words — so you must actually understand it.

② Oral exam (Wk 13–14)
I ask you about your own contribution to the team project — what you built, why, and where AI helped.
Every member is asked individually. This protects you from carrying — or being carried by — your team.

Every class

The last 10 minutes: AI Critique

Every chapter class ends with a 10-minute exercise. I project something an AI wrote that contains a mistake. You find it, explain it, and fix it — by hand on A4 paper.

Why
Catching an AI's mistake is exactly the skill this course is about — and it is the one thing you cannot ask the AI to do for you.
Intro2SE · Day 1 Orientation
How to answer

Three steps — every time

  • 1. Spot the error — point to exactly what the AI got wrong
  • 2. Explain why — one or two sentences
  • 3. Fix it — write the corrected version, or draw the correct diagram
Bring every class
✏️ A4 paper + a pen. Name + student ID + week number at the top.
Marks
1 = spotted + explained + fixed
0.5 = spotted only
0 = blank / not submitted
