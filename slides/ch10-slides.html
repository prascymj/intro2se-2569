Concept 1 / 5

Software engineering ethics

Engineers have duties beyond “make it work”: act in the public interest, be honest, protect users. Professional codes (ACM/IEEE) spell this out.

Example
If your app could harm users (lost data, leaked info), you must raise it — even if it slows the project.
Intro2SE · Ch10
Concept 2 / 5

Data ethics & privacy

Just because you CAN collect data doesn't mean you should. Collect the minimum, protect it, and be transparent about its use.

Example
StudyMate doesn't need a student's ID card number to remind them of deadlines — so don't ask for it.
Intro2SE · Ch10
Concept 3 / 5

Responsible AI use

AI can be biased or wrong, and it cannot be accountable — a person must sign off. Be transparent about where AI was used.

Example
An AI that suggests study times shouldn't quietly favor some students; a human reviews its suggestions. Your AI Log makes use transparent.
Intro2SE · Ch10
Concept 4 / 5

Professional practice

Communicate clearly, meet commitments, give and take feedback, and document your work. These habits matter as much as code.

Example
Showing up, submitting on time, and a readable README are professional behavior (and part of your grade).
Intro2SE · Ch10
Concept 5 / 5

Tools & development environments

Real teams use an IDE, version control, automated tests, and a way to deploy. Basic CI runs your tests automatically on every push.

Example
Push to GitHub → CI runs your unit tests → green check means it's safe to merge.
