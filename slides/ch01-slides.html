Concept 1 / 5

Software engineering is a discipline, not just coding

SE is applying an organized, team-based process to build software that works, can be changed, and ships on time. The code is only one part — understanding needs, designing, testing, and maintaining matter just as much.

Example
Writing a script to rename your files = programming. Building an app many people rely on, that a team maintains for years = software engineering.

Concept 2 / 5

Why software projects fail

Most failures are NOT bad code. They come from building the wrong thing, poor planning, weak teamwork, or no testing. The whole point of a “process” is to prevent these failures.

Example
A team builds a beautiful app… that nobody uses, because they never asked real users what they needed.
Intro2SE · Ch1
Concept 3 / 5

Quality is more than “it runs”

Good software must be usable, reliable, maintainable, secure, and efficient — not just produce output once. These are called quality attributes.

Example
StudyMate that sometimes loses your tasks (unreliable) or shows other students your schedule (insecure) is low quality even if it “works” in a demo.
Intro2SE · Ch1
Concept 4 / 5

The software process at a glance

Five activities repeat through every project: Specify (what) → Design (how) → Build → Validate (test) → Evolve (change). This whole course walks you through them once, as a team.

Example
StudyMate reminder: specify “remind 1 day before” → design a scheduler → code it → test it → improve it next sprint.
Intro2SE · Ch1
Concept 5 / 5

The human in the AI era + the course rule

AI can generate code, but it cannot decide what is worth building, judge trade-offs, or take responsibility. That is YOUR job: you direct AI and you verify it. So the rule is: log it, verify it, explain it.

Example
AI writes a login function. You decide whether storing passwords that way is safe — and you own the result. In the oral exam you must explain it.
