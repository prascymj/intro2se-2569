The Golden Thread (how the project is judged) 🧵

We grade coherence, not quantity. Everything must connect:

Problem → Requirement → Design → Feature → Test must be one consistent thing.

A small project where the thread holds beats a big one where the pieces don't connect. You'll keep a simple Traceability Matrix (one row per feature) — see Project.

AI Policy (read carefully) 🤖

You may use AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.). This course is AI-First. But:

  1. Log every use — keep an AI Usage Log: what you asked, what AI gave, what you changed.
  2. Verify it — AI is often wrong. Check and fix its output against your requirements/tests.
  3. Explain it — in the oral exam you must explain any part you submitted, including AI-assisted parts.

❌ Not allowed: submitting AI output you don't understand; passing off a teammate's work as yours; hiding AI use. ✅ Encouraged: using AI to learn faster, draft, and explore — then making it your own and being able to defend it.

What happens if you can't explain your work? That part scores 0 in the oral exam, and undisclosed AI use becomes an academic-integrity case.

Two individual checks

① The assignment (10%, due Week 8). Pick one article from the GitHub Resources library, make a one-page summary and a ≤3-minute video of you presenting it. You have to say it out loud in your own words — so you have to actually understand it.

② The oral exam (10%, Weeks 13–14). We ask you about your own contribution to the team project: what you built, why you chose it, and where you used AI and what you changed. Every member is asked individually — so free-riding and "AI wrote it" both show up immediately. → What to submit
